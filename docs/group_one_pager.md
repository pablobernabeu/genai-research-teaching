# Quick start — your group's note

*One copy or two per table. You can do all of this without looking at the screen.*

## 1 · Open the app

### `genai-rt.web.app`

Type that into any browser, **or scan the QR on screen**. No account, nothing to install.

## 2 · Start your group — one person does this

**One form does both.** A blank join code starts a new group (needs the passcode); a join code filled in joins an existing one (no passcode needed).

- **Group name** — choose one that does **not identify anyone** (e.g. "Otters", "Team Kelp").
- **Session passcode** — type the one the facilitator reads out (needed only to start a group).
- Press **Continue** — the app shows a **6-character join code**.
- *(Optional)* Read the code out so teammates join on their own devices. One shared device is perfectly fine.

**To join from another device:** open the same address, type the **same** group name, paste the join code, and **leave the passcode field empty** — only the first person needs the passcode.

*Stuck?* If the passcode is rejected, check it with the facilitator. If the name is taken, pick another. Joining and it says no such group? Check the spelling, or leave the code blank to create it instead.

## 3 · Choose a scenario

Pick the track you are working on from the dropdown:

- **A — Methodological Blind-Spot Detector**
- **B — Executive-Function Layer**
- **C — Rapid Prototyping**
- **D — Public Engagement**
- **Own problem** — a real one someone brings: anonymise, synthesise or abstain; never personal, student, unpublished or confidential data.

## 4 · The core three — the heart of the fifteen minutes

If this is all you manage, you have done the task.

1. **One artefact move** — run your tool on your problem and make something: a critique, a template, a small artefact, a translation.
2. **One or two caught errors** — moments it was fluent and wrong. Keep them — this is your *museum of caught errors*.
3. **One insight** — the limitation, the safeguard, or one honest thing this exposed — the line you will give in the 45-second lightning round.

**If you have about five minutes left, go deeper:**

- **Automation–steering map** — which steps the tool ran, and where *you* steered: decided, verified or overrode.
- **Oversight model** — **interwoven** (checking throughout) or **staged** (check at set points)? A box follows to say why, and what it cost.
- **Field reflection** *(wrap-up)* — is your field over- or under-using GenAI here, with what consequences, and how would you redress it over the next two years?

## 5 · Share and submit

- **Share** *(optional)* — tick the consent box if your group is happy for its **non-identifying** submission to appear in the public workshop archive. Leave it unticked to keep the note private to the facilitator.
- **Submit** — pick a scenario first, or the app will ask you to. Submit when you are done — ideally by the time we stop building (around 13:30).
- Once you submit, the form goes **read-only** while the facilitator reviews it. If they reopen it (with a note on what to fix), you can edit again and press **Resubmit**. Nothing is lost.

## Good to know

- **Data safety** — no personal or confidential data goes into a free AI tool. When in doubt, **anonymise, synthesise or abstain**. The only identifier the app holds is your group name.
- **Your work is safe** — the app saves your changes automatically. Reloaded the page or lost Wi-Fi? Your note is held on this device and syncs when you reconnect.
- **The timer** — a calm chip in the corner shows the time remaining. It is **advisory only** — no red, no flashing, no sound — and it never locks you out.
- **Optional roles** — share these angles, or ignore them entirely: **Convenor** (problem and clock) · **Driver** (the keyboard) · **Steward** (the red lines) · **Reporter** (the note and 45-second line) · **Sceptic** (break the output).
- **If the app will not load** — use the **HackMD** link or paper the facilitator points you to; same headings, you will not lose your reasoning. On HackMD, share the note link with the facilitator (the same as submitting in the app).
