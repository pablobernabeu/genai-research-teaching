# Facilitator cue cards (print-ready)

The holdable subset of the [facilitator guide](../workshop_plan.md): one card per
moment, with the line to say and the thing to watch. Cut along the rules. Times are
wall-clock — the workshop is two 30-minute parts split by lunch.

---

### ✂︎ — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — —

## 12:00 · WELCOME + STANCE (Part 1)

- **Say:** "We are here for *discernment, not enthusiasm* — a deeper insight into the
  technology, not training on any one tool. Whatever your confidence, you are welcome."
- **Do:** read the disclaimer aloud; land *friction is a signal*.
- **Watch:** start on time; this is the conceptual part — keep it brisk.

### ✂︎ — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — —

## 12:03 · SAVED OR BURNED

- **Say:** "One sentence each with your neighbour: the last time an AI tool *saved
  you* or *burned you*."
- **Do:** two minutes, in parallel, then pull the room back.

### ✂︎ — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — —

## 12:05 · A LONG LINE OF TOOLS

- **Say:** "AI is the latest in a long line of productivity tools — calculator,
  computer, spreadsheet, now generative AI — in an economy that rewards output. Not
  whether to use it, but how, with discernment."
- **Do:** the chain on screen; a couple of minutes.

### ✂︎ — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — —

## 12:08 · GETTING STARTED (no experience needed)

- **Say:** "A chat assistant: you type a request, it writes back. Pick one free tool;
  say who you are, what you want and what good looks like, then push back."
- **Do:** point to the starter prompts; reassure — mixed groups are a strength, no
  one is behind.

### ✂︎ — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — —

## 12:10 · FRICTION IS A SIGNAL

- **Say:** "When the tool resists you, that friction is information — it marks where
  your judgement does the work."
- **Do:** Lee et al. and Drosos et al. in a sentence each. Do not lecture.

### ✂︎ — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — —

## 12:14 · SPECTRUM · RED LINES · ETHICS

- **Do:** one breath each (slides 10–12). Name the teaching angle: students' data,
  assessment integrity, disclosure.
- **Watch:** these are reference frames for Part 2, not content to master now.

### ✂︎ — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — —

## 12:21 · THE BIGGER QUESTIONS + HUMAN-IN-THE-LOOP

- **Say:** "Zoom out: the thinking/writing shift; fairness — who is helped, who is
  left out (language, support, career stage, background); and disclosure. No answers
  — notice them in Part 2."
- **Do:** then human-in-the-loop and the map (slide 14): accountability stays human.

### ✂︎ — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — —

## 12:26 · WHAT PART 2 ASKS + FORM GROUPS (end of Part 1)

- **Say:** "These ideas — and the bigger questions — are the rubric and reflection
  you will fill. Now: groups of five, gather at the idea on screen; the letter is
  your track."
- **Do:** preview the rubric (slide 15) and the tracks; then to lunch. End Part 1 at
  12:30.

### ✂︎ — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — —

## 12:30–13:15 · NETWORKING LUNCH (not your session)

- **Do:** a networking break; Part 2 at 13:15.

### ✂︎ — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — —

## 13:15 · SETTLE IN (Part 2)

- **Say:** "Re-find your group. Quickly agree your **one** problem — the seed, or a real
  one someone brought. Open the app (genai-rt.web.app); read out the passcode. Red lines on."
- **Do:** about three minutes in all — regroup, agree the one problem in roughly a minute, then open the app and read out the passcode. Keep it tight so it does not eat into the fifteen-minute applied session.

### ✂︎ — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — —

## 13:18 · APPLY IT (15 min)

- **Let them work.** Stop sensitive data; unstick stalls; one provocation each.
- **Push:** the **automation–steering map** — interwoven or staged? Keep one caught
  error.

### ✂︎ — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — —

## ~13:26 · PIVOT ⏱

- **Say:** "Thirty seconds, keyboards down. What did it make easier, and at what
  cost? Where did it resist you? Held the red lines? Now switch: building →
  interrogating."

### ✂︎ — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — —

## 13:33 · LIGHTNING ROUND (7 min)

- **Say:** "45 seconds, one insight, no slides: the biggest limitation, or the best
  safeguard."
- **Do:** visible timer; 45 s each for as many as fit, then a one-line harvest from
  the rest. Jot each group's thread; approve their note on the dashboard as it lands.

### ✂︎ — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — — —

## 13:40 · CLOSE (5 min)

- **Synthesise, don't summarise:** name two or three threads from the round.
- **Land:** friction as signal · the spectrum · the red lines · human-in-the-loop.
- **Give:** the take-home actions; further reading; the follow-up. Close at 13:45.
