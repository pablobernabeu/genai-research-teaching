What am I doing here? — tap to re-read at any point

In ~15 minutes, get the core three done first:

  1. One artefact move — run your tool on your problem and keep what is useful.
  2. One caught error — a moment it was fluent and wrong. Keep it.
  3. One insight — the limitation, or the safeguard, for the 45-second round.

If you have ~5 minutes left: sketch the automation–steering map (which steps the tool ran, where you steered) and decide whether oversight was interwoven or staged.

Red lines — never paste personal data, students' marks or notes, unpublished or participant data, or confidential/embargoed material. When in doubt: anonymise, synthesise or abstain.

The four tracks — pick one
  • A · Methodological Blind-Spot Detector — turn the tool on your own study or design and verify which of its critiques are real. The purest test of the day's thesis.
  • B · Executive-Function Layer — build a small, reusable aid for planning, triage or accessibility (e.g. messy notes → owned actions).
  • C · Rapid Prototyping (stretch) — make a tiny artefact (a script, a chart, an explainer), then check it. Beware the "vibe-coding trap": it runs and looks right but is subtly wrong. You will not fully verify in 15 minutes — the verifying is the point.
  • D · Public Engagement Translator — turn a finding into a lay summary or explainer, then audit it for fidelity (what got distorted?).
  • Own problem — a real, non-confidential problem a member brings. Anonymise or synthesise first.
The five roles — take one each (or share)
  • Convenor — keeps the real problem and the clock in focus.
  • Driver — operates the tools, owns the tool choices.
  • Steward — guards the red lines (use the data check below).
  • Reporter — owns the note, the link and the 45-second insight.
  • Sceptic — tries to break the output, curates the caught errors.

About where attention goes, not who owns the work — rotate the keyboard freely. An experienced group can ignore these.

Can I paste this? — the data check

Walk four questions before anything goes into a free tool:

  1. Personal data? (identifies a living person) → anonymise first; check your lawful basis.
  2. Special-category or children's data? (health, ethnicity, beliefs, biometrics) → stop; synthesise or abstain.
  3. Confidential, unpublished or embargoed? (grants, peer review, NDA, no consent) → stop; not yours alone to waive.
  4. Do you hold the rights? If unsure, treat it as restricted.

If "no" to any: anonymise · synthesise · abstract up a level · or abstain (a valid finding for your museum).
